How it works

Three steps, no account, no upload

Everything runs locally in your browser using Google's MediaPipe Pose model. There's no server compute — the video is analyzed on your own hardware and never sent anywhere.

Step 1

Film your ride

Camera square-on at hip height, pedaling steadily for 20–30 seconds. A turbo trainer is ideal. Upload a clip or record with your webcam on the spot.

Step 2

AI tracks your joints

The pose model finds your shoulder, elbow, wrist, hip, knee and ankle in every frame, locks onto your near side, and skips joints it can't clearly see.

Step 3

Get graded advice

Your angles at the bottom of the pedal stroke are graded against research ranges, with a plain-English fix for anything out of range — one change at a time.

The measurements

Side view — the core fit read

Angles are sampled at the bottom of every pedal stroke and reported as the median across the whole clip, so a single blurry frame can't skew the result. Each is graded green / amber / red against ranges from the Holmes method and dynamic-fit research.

Side view Primary

The reliable read. Saddle height (knee angle) is the number to trust most; reach-related angles are softer on a 2D view.

What it measuresTargetReliability
Knee at bottom of strokeSaddle height. Too bent = saddle low; nearly straight = saddle high. You get a specific mm adjustment. 30–40° High
Torso from horizontalHow aggressive your position is — front-end height and stack. 40–50° Medium
Elbow bendLocked-out vs. softened arms — comfort and shock absorption. 15–30° Medium
Shoulder angle (reach)How stretched-out you are to the bars. Treated cautiously — don't swap a stem on one clip. 80–95° Soft
Hip at top of strokeInformational reference — depends heavily on flexibility and reach, so it's shown but not graded. 85–110° Info

Front & rear views Beta

Frontal-plane hints, filmed facing the camera or from directly behind. These are directional cues, not verdicts — for real decisions the side-view saddle-height read is the trustworthy one.

What it measuresTells youReliability
Knee tracking (FPPA)Frontal-plane projection angle — whether your knee dives in or bows out through the stroke. Cleat / stability cues Beta
Pelvic rockPeak-to-peak tilt of your hip line across the stroke — often a saddle-too-high sign. Saddle height / stability Beta
Left/right symmetryWhether the two sides move evenly. Imbalance cues Beta

More features

Built to be easy and private

Three ways to add your clip

  • Drag & drop a video file
  • Browse and pick from your device (.mp4, .mov, .webm)
  • Record straight from your webcam, up to 30 seconds

Nothing leaves your device

All pose detection and math run in your browser. There's no upload, no account, and no server compute — so your footage stays yours.

Fast, with a safe fallback

Uses your GPU via WebGPU when available and automatically falls back to CPU/WASM on older browsers, so it works widely.

Catches bad footage

If your clip isn't square-on — which stretches or squashes the angles, especially reach — it warns you to re-film instead of trusting a skewed reading.

⚠

Basic fitting for everyone — not a replacement for a pro fitter. A perfect fit still needs a professional and their hands-on judgement. What this tool does is make basic fitting accessible: a quick, free way to catch the obvious stuff (saddle far too high or low, wildly off angles) before you ever book a session. It's a 2D side-view estimate, not a 3D pro fit, and it's still a work in progress — actively looking for input from qualified bike fitters to sharpen its ranges and advice. If you're a fitter (or know one), feedback is very welcome — open an issue on GitHub. Treat the results as a starting point, change one thing at a time, and re-film. Not medical advice.
