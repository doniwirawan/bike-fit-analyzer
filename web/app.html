Do this

    2D side-view estimate, not a 3D pro fit. Saddle height (knee) is the most reliable number here; reach/shoulder is the softest — don't change your stem on one clip. Change one thing at a time, then re-film.
    Front view · beta
    ⓘ Frontal-plane readings are experimental and less reliable than the side view. Treat them as directional hints, not verdicts.
    Knee tracking uses the frontal projection angle (FPPA); pelvic rock is peak-to-peak hip-line tilt across the stroke. For real decisions, the side-view saddle-height (knee) reading is the trustworthy one.

    Square-on

    Point the camera square to the view you picked — dead side-on, front-on, or straight behind. Off-axis skews the angles.

    Hip height

    Phone at hip/crank height on something stable — nothing blocking your legs (or arms, side-on).

    Pedal steady

    20–30s at an easy, normal cadence — a trainer is ideal. Good light = less blur.
